Ph.D. THESIS ABSTRACT:

Thesis title: Ab initio Quantum Chemical Studies on Kinetic Fractionation during analysis of Carbonates for the Clumped Isotope Thermometry.

Abstract: In stable isotope geochemistry, liberated CO2 from the acid-carbonate reaction are used in a mass spectrometer to analyze the carbon and oxygen isotopic compositions of the product CO2, to obtain the carbon and oxygen isotopic compositions of the carbonate. The stable and clumped isotopic compositions of the carbonate indicate the formation temperature of the same. As only two out of the three oxygen atom per carbonate molecule, contributes to the product CO2 and there are possible rearrangements among carbon and oxygen isotopic bonds during the reaction, we studied the acid-carbonate reaction mechanism based on protonation and calculated the associated clumped isotope fractionation using ab initio quantum chemical method, partition function method and transition state theory to correlate the isotopic distribution from product to the reactant.

We tracked down at which step of the reaction the heavier carbon (13C) and oxygen (18O) isotopes are forming the clumped isotopic bond that contributes to the product CO2. The amount of clumping mainly depends on the reaction temperature, and the dependency is calculated using the partition function theory, where the frequencies are obtained from ab initio quantum chemical calculations. A script in ’python’ is written for calculating the temperature dependency of clumped isotope fractionation. Using transition state theory, different isomers of the transition state having one imaginary frequency for the same reactants were found using Gaussian ’QST2’ and ’QST3’ calculations. We calculated the path at both sides of the transition state using the intrinsic reaction coordinates (IRC) method to verify the saddle point. The entire reaction was studied in the ’solvent as water’ using a polarizable continuum model (PCM) to mimic the experimental conditions. For that, we used self-consistent reaction field calculations in PCM, where the gas phase reaction was performed in a cavity having a dielectric constant ϵ = 78.3553.

We showed that the acid digestion reaction, which results in the formation of CO2 enriched with 13C -18O bonds, yielded a relationship of acid fractionation factor (AFF): Δ47AFF calcite = -0.30175 + 0.57700 * (105∕T2) - 0.10791 * (105∕T2)2. Transition state structure, obtained in our study, includes the cations present in the carbonate minerals and provides distinct acid fractionation factors for calcite and dolomite (Δ47AFF dolomite = -0.28563 + 0.49508 * (105∕T2) - 0.08231 * (105∕T2)2) that determines the effect of the cations present in the carbonate mineral lattice on acid fractionation factor. The theoretical slope of the dolomite-acid digestion curve is lower than the calcite-acid digestion curve, which is discussed considering the stronger Mg-O bond.
